LXAI + BAI at ICLR 2019

The LatinX in AI Coalition is partnering with Black in AI to host a joint workshop at the Seventh International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR) in New Orleans May 7th, 2019 at the Ernest N. Morial Convention Center.

Mission

Our mission is to increase representation of our communities at the main conference and create a platform allowing our members to share their work, gain access to mentoring, collaborations, and networking opportunities with other researchers in artificial intelligence.

Call for Participation

The workshop is a one-day event with invited speakers, oral presentations, and posters. The event brings together faculty, graduate students, research scientists, and engineers for an opportunity to connect and exchange ideas. There will be a panel discussion and a mentoring session to discuss current research trends and career choices in artificial intelligence and machine learning. While all presenters will identify primarily as latinx and black, all are invited to attend.

Submission Instructions

We strongly encourage students, post-docs and researchers who primarily identify as latinx and/or black in all areas of machine learning to submit an abstract describing new, previously, or concurrently published research. We welcome abstract submissions, in theory, methodology, as well as applications. Abstracts may describe completed research or work-in-progress. The presenting author need not be the first author of the work.

Authors of accepted abstracts will be asked to present their work in a poster session. A few authors will be selected to give 15 minute oral presentations. Submissions will be peer-reviewed. Authors are encouraged to sign up to review for LXAI & BAI, with a sign-up option available upon submission.

Submission Guidelines

Submissions may be up to two pages including all figures and tables, with an additional page for references submitted as a link to a PDF. The submissions should be in a single column. They should be typeset using 11-point or larger fonts and should have at least 1-inch margin all around. Submissions that do not follow these guidelines risk being rejected without consideration of their merits. Submissions must state the research problem, motivation, and technical contribution. Submissions must be self-contained and include all figures, tables, and references.

The submission deadline is 11:59 PM (PST), March 8th, 2019. Please note that no extensions will be offered for submissions.

Attending ICLR

Please notes that an abstract acceptance in this workshop and/or registration to attend this workshop does not guarantee access to ICLR. You have to register to attend ICLR separately ( https://iclr.cc/Register/view-registration).

As a result, we would like to inform you that ICLR registration will sell out quickly. If you are planning to attend ICLR, we want to encourage you to register now. You can cancel your registration for a full refund until March 19th if you end up not being able to attend. ( https://iclr.cc/Help/CancellationPolicy).

We also encourage you to book your accommodation as the discounted room blocks are being filled up quickly.
